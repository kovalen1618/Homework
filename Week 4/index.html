Single-Origin Coffee

Coffee comes from many different places - but most on the grocery shelf tend to be a blend of origins from around the world. That's not to say these coffees taste awful, but there are certain characteristics that shine through in coffees from specific farms in specific countries - not to mention the idea of keeping local coffee farmers supported for their hard work.

My Favorite Coffees

Here is a list of single-origin coffees that I have found to be exceptional ranked highest to lowest:

  1. Bies Panantan, Indonesia - a medium roast with notes of dark chocolate and balanced by tropical fruit acidity. Farmed by a dedicated women-led cooperative on the northern-side of Sumatra.
  2. Hueheutenango, Guatemala - an STL local bagger working with Guatemala farmers to disperse a complex and creamy coffee with notes of raspberry, cherry, and caramel. Farmed in the western highlands of Guatemala.
  3. Nueva Segovia, Nicaragua - while this particular bagger doesn't sell this coffee on demand, and instead curate different batches for each month, this particular brew was exceptional. Hailing from volcanic rich soil in Nicaragua - this medium roast contained notes of butter, black cherry, and toffee.
